Project overview

From cleaned sales records to executive-ready business insight.

This project analyzes Northstar Beverage Distribution's 2022 retail performance across retailers, brands, regions, states, pricing, expenses, and time. The goal is to make the business evidence reproducible, auditable, and easy for management to act on.

Pipeline design

ETL steps that separate raw, cleaned, analytical, and presentation layers.

The repository separates the raw, cleaned, analytical, and presentation layers so every output can be regenerated from the cleaned source file.

01

Extract

Read source data from the cleaned beverage CSV containing retailer, date, geography, brand, price, units, revenue, and expenses.

02

Transform

Validate required fields, cast numeric columns, and derive profit, margin, month, quarter, and weighted price.

03

Load

Generate reusable CSV and JSON outputs by month, quarter, retailer, brand, region, state, and priority combinations.

04

Present

Create an executive Markdown report and a PowerPoint deck that summarize findings and recommendations.

Business questions

What the project demonstrates.

The analysis covers commercial performance, retailer and brand mix, and geographic insight to support data-driven decision-making.

01

Commercial performance

Tracks revenue, expenses, contribution profit, margin, units sold, and weighted selling price over the 2022 review period.

02

Retailer and brand mix

Identifies account concentration, brand contribution, pricing differences, and retailer-brand combinations that deserve review.

03

Geographic insight

Compares regions and states to show where revenue, unit volume, price, and margin patterns differ across the market.

04

Quality checks

Checks for missing values, exact duplicates, negative values, zero activity, formula variance, and repeated apparent keys.

05

Responsible analysis

Flags unusual records for validation without automatically deleting rows that may represent valid separate business activity.

06

Known gaps

The dataset lacks transaction, store, SKU, promotion, inventory, shipment, and expense-category fields, so causal claims are avoided.
