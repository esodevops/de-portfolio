Project overview

From raw banking files to analytics-ready models.

ZuloBank transforms a denormalized banking dataset into clean, purpose-built data products. The workflow separates customer, account, transaction, and loan concerns while building focused warehouses for transaction and loan analytics.

The challenge

One crowded banking file needs purpose-built structure.

The raw dataset mixes customers, accounts, transactions, and loans. ZuloBank separates those concerns, preserves their relationships, and reshapes them for faster business analysis.

01

Denormalized source

Repeated customer and account attributes create duplication and make changes harder to control.

02

Incomplete loan data

Missing loan fields need consistent treatment before records can support trustworthy reporting.

03

Mixed analytical grains

Transactions and loans describe different business events and need purpose-built fact tables.

04

Time-based reporting

A reusable date dimension is required for daily, monthly, quarterly, and annual analysis.

05

Clear fact grains

Loan and transaction models keep measures aligned with their true business events.

06

Portable outputs

Generated CSV tables can be inspected directly or loaded into a database platform.

Transformation workflow

From raw CSV to analytics-ready models.

A transparent Jupyter and Pandas workflow makes each preparation and modeling step easy to inspect and rerun.

01

Ingest

Load the raw ZuloBank CSV and inspect its structure, types, and missing values.

02

Clean

Repair loan-related gaps and split full customer names into atomic fields.

03

Normalize

Separate repeated attributes into customer, account, transaction, and loan tables.

04

Enrich

Generate a comprehensive calendar dimension for consistent time analysis.

05

Model

Build focused transaction and loan star schemas and export every table to CSV.

Database design

Five connected banking tables.

The normalized model separates core entities while retaining the keys needed to connect every transaction to its customer, account, and loan context.

customersIdentity and contact details
accountsBanking accounts and balances
transactionsDeposits and withdrawals
loansLoan amounts, status, and terms
date_dimensionCalendar for time analysis
