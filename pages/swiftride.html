Project overview

Replace manual records with a reliable operating model.

SwiftRide needed a single source of truth for customers, orders, deliveries, fleet resources, inventory, and payments. This project transforms fragmented logistics data into a governed, queryable operations platform with end-to-end referential integrity.

Technical architecture

Four schemas, one connected operational view.

The architecture separates business domains while preserving cross-schema relationships through primary keys, foreign keys, validation constraints, and cascade rules.

01

Operations Schema

Customers, orders, and deliveries form the core transaction workflow with full referential integrity.

02

Fleet Schema

Drivers and vehicles support assignment, status tracking, and capacity analysis.

03

Warehouse Schema

Warehouse locations and inventory quantities enable real-time stock monitoring.

04

Finance Schema

Payments connect financial status directly to customer orders with audit trails.

05

Data Governance

Role-based access controls and environment-based credentials protect sensitive data.

06

Automated Loading

One-command Supabase loader handles schema init, CSV imports, and validation.

Delivery workflow

From raw CSV files to management decisions.

I treated the project as an operational pipeline, not a collection of isolated SQL exercises. Each stage builds on the previous to deliver production-ready outcomes.

01

Model

Translated business requirements into normalized entities and relationships.

02

Govern

Applied keys, uniqueness, checks, cascading rules, and role-based access controls.

03

Load

Automated foreign-key-safe CSV ingestion into Supabase PostgreSQL.

04

Analyze

Built reusable DQL for revenue, delivery, customer, fleet, payment, and inventory analysis.

05

Validate

Post-load row-count validation and referential integrity checks confirm data quality.

06

Report

Generated Markdown and color PDF reports for technical and management audiences.

Database design

Eight connected operational tables.

The normalized model separates core entities while retaining the keys needed to connect every order to its customer, delivery, fleet assignment, and payment outcome.

customersIdentity and contact details
ordersOrder headers with values and dates
deliveriesDelivery status and tracking
driversDriver info and assignments
vehiclesFleet capacity and status
warehousesStorage locations
inventoryStock levels and products
paymentsTransaction status and amounts
